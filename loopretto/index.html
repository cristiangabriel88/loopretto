00 · The whole idea

01 · Get a track in

Paste a link, or just drop the file.

Paste a YouTube link and Loopretto fetches the audio for you anonymously, with no login and no cookies. SoundCloud, Bandcamp, Vimeo and direct audio URLs work too. Already have the file? Drag it straight onto the window and it never touches a server at all. And once a track is loaded, one click downloads the MP3 to your own machine, so you can keep the audio for offline practice.

  • Allowlisted sources: YouTube, SoundCloud, Bandcamp, Vimeo + their subdomains, or any direct audio-file URL.
  • Anonymous downloads: no account, no cookies, nothing to sign into.
  • Download the MP3: save the fetched audio to your computer, keeping the track's real title.
  • Drag-and-drop local files play instantly via an in-browser object URL, with zero round-trip to any server.
  • Leading section + size caps keep fetches quick and lean.

02 · The loop

Click the waveform. That's your loop.

The first move in any transcription. A crisp WaveSurfer waveform fills the screen. Click anywhere to set your start point, hit Loop, and a region appears. Drag its edges to frame exactly that difficult part you're trying to catch, and it repeats seamlessly, forever, while your ear chips away at it note by note.

  • Click-to-set start point, then a draggable, resizable loop region.
  • Seamless repeat that jumps back the instant the region ends.
  • Rep counter: a × N badge ticks up every time the loop comes around.
  • Wheel-zoom into the waveform for sample-tight loop points; ←/→ nudge by half a second.
  • Streamed playback via HTTP range requests, so the track starts instantly, with no full download wait.

03 · Tempo & pitch, decoupled

Slow it down to get it down.

The transcriber's secret weapon. Pull the speed back to 50% and a blistering run unspools slow enough to hear every note; pitch stays put, no chipmunk, no warble. Tap Key −/+ to transpose up to a full octave while the tempo never moves, so you can match the part to your own instrument with one click.

  • Speed control preserves pitch: slow practice that still sounds musical.
  • Transpose −12…+12 semitones with the Key buttons; tempo unchanged.
  • A live-stream pitch shifter that's cleanly bypassed at 0 semitones for untouched audio.
  • Both work on the streaming player: no re-decode, no waiting.

04 · Channel isolation

Isolate the instrument you're transcribing.

When the part you want is buried in the mix, a four-way switch reroutes the stereo field in real time. Solo the left or right channel to lift a panned instrument out so your ear can lock onto it, or flip to Karaoke, which plays the left-minus-right difference to cancel center-panned vocals and expose the backing underneath.

  • Stereo · L · R · Karaoke: one segmented control, instant switching.
  • Karaoke mode plays the mid-side difference to drop center vocals.
  • Resets to clean Stereo on every new track automatically.
  • Built on a live Web Audio splitter/merger graph: no file processing.

05 · Sound shaping

Three studio effects, on tap.

Open the Sound panel and sculpt the tone before it reaches your ears. Each effect lives in the audio chain only while it's switched on, with sliders that move the sound live.

Name your file screenshot-05.png: the Sound FX panel.

📊

Parametric EQ

A 5-band filter chain (80 Hz · 250 · 1k · 3.5k · 12k) with ready-made presets: boost the low end, scoop the mud, or bypass it entirely.

🔦

Band-pass spotlight

A single sweepable band-pass with log-mapped Freq and Width sliders to narrow in on one frequency band and isolate a riff hiding in the mix.

🥊

Punch

A compressor plus make-up gain to even out dynamics and push a quiet part forward so every note reads clearly.

06 · Live note detection

A second opinion for your ear.

Stuck on a note you can't quite name? Flip on the Notes overlay and Loopretto listens to the playing audio, runs autocorrelation pitch detection, and shows the note in real time (name plus how many cents sharp or flat), with a rolling strip of the recently detected pitches. The fastest way to confirm what you're hearing and get that difficult part down on paper.

  • Live note pill: note name + cents deviation, updated every animation frame.
  • Rolling history strip of recently detected, stable pitches.
  • Reads the analyser tap on the master output, so it works with speed/key changes applied.

07 · Metronome

A click that actually stays in time.

A look-ahead scheduler keeps the click rock-solid. Tap the tempo in, dial beats-per-bar, and watch the beat dots pulse. Set beats-per-bar to 1 for a plain unaccented pulse, or higher to put an accent on the downbeat.

  • Tap tempo: set BPM by feel, or nudge it with ±.
  • Accented downbeat with adjustable beats-per-bar (1 = no accent).
  • Visual beat dots light in sync with the audible click.
  • Routes straight to output, so it's unaffected by your speed, key, or FX settings, and you can swap the click sample by dropping in your own WAV.

08 · Drone

A reference pitch to check your ear against.

Working out a key centre or testing intonation while you transcribe? The Drone panel sustains up to three sine voices (pick a note and octave, optionally add the octave below) through a gentle lowpass and its own volume control. Hold a tonic underneath the track and the notes you're chasing snap into context. It bypasses the master output, so transpose and the memorize fade never touch your reference tone.

  • Up to three sustained voices: note + octave, with an optional octave-below.
  • Its own lowpass filter and volume, independent of the track.
  • Bypasses master gain: stays steady through transpose and memorize mode.

09 · Fretboard

Find the note on the neck.

Heard the pitch but not sure where it lives on the fretboard? A full neck is rendered right in the page, thickest string on the bottom, just like you're looking down at the instrument. Click any position to hear it from real recorded samples, match it to the track, and every spot sharing that pitch class lights up, so transcribing by ear turns straight into a fingering.

  • Guitar / Bass switch, plus a Labels toggle, both remembered between visits.
  • Click to play from real recorded samples, pitch-shifted to the nearest anchor (with a synth fallback).
  • Pitch-class highlighting: every matching position on the neck lights up.
  • Routes straight to output, like the metronome and drone.

10 · Reference piano

Sing it, then find it on the keys.

A reference piano sits right in the player, voiced with real recorded samples. Click the keys or use the keyboard (z x c v b n m for the white keys and s d g h j for the black) to check a pitch against the track and pin down exactly which note you're hearing, without ever leaving the page.

  • Real mp3 piano samples across a chromatic octave.
  • Keyboard-mapped: white keys on z x c v b n m, sharps on s d g h j.
  • Always available alongside the waveform for quick pitch checks.

11 · Practice tools

It keeps score, so you can just play.

Loopretto isn't only a player; it's a practice log. Everything below is keyed to the current song and saved locally in your browser, building a quiet record of the work you put in.

Name your file screenshot-11.png: practice session + journal.

Session stopwatch & journal

A manual wall-clock stopwatch you start and stop; it counts whether the track is playing or paused, so working a part out on the instrument still counts. Stop a session and it's logged with the time, your reps, optional notes, and any pomodoros, then rolled into a per-song journal.

  • Wall-clock timer, not a playback clock: credits real practice time.
  • Journaled sessions with notes, reps and pomodoro counts per song.
  • Survives changing tracks, and banks your time even if you just close the tab.
🎯

Daily goal & streak

Set a daily target (30 min by default) and watch a progress bar fill against today's total. A streak counter tallies consecutive days you've put in the work.

🍅

Pomodoro

An independent focus/break countdown (25/5 by default). Each phase change chimes a soft ping and pops a toast: no asset, no notification permission needed.

🔁

Loop reps

Every time your loop comes back around, the rep counter ticks up: a running tally of how many times you've drilled this exact phrase today.

🧠

Memorize mode

Each loop pass quietly steps the track volume down a few dB until it fades to silence, then back up, weaning you off the recording so your memory takes over.

Goals, streaks & focus, at a glance

The daily goal bar, the streak count and the pomodoro timer all live together: a small dashboard that makes consistency visible without nagging you. Everything is stored in localStorage, on your machine, under your control.

12 · Setlists & resume

Pick up exactly where you left off.

Group the songs you're working on into named setlists, shown on the loading screen; click one to load it instantly. And every few seconds Loopretto quietly remembers your last session, so a "Resume" banner offers to bring back the track, loop, speed, key and zoom right where you stopped. It never auto-downloads, so you stay in control of when a fetch happens.

  • Named setlists of your URL-based songs, one click to load.
  • Resume banner restores loop, speed, pitch and zoom from last time.
  • Opt-in re-fetch: you click Resume; nothing downloads behind your back.
  • All stored locally in your browser: your library, your machine.

The whole point

Your files. Your computer. Your control.

Loopretto is built to run on localhost and nowhere else. There's no account to create, no server to upload to, no analytics, no telemetry. The audio you load, the loops you set, the practice you log: none of it ever leaves the machine you're sitting at. This isn't a free tier that gets monetized later; it's free and open source, and local-only is the design, permanently.

01

No login, ever

No sign-up, no email, no password. Open it and play. Even downloads run anonymously: no account, no cookies.

02

No cloud storage

Nothing is uploaded. Tracks are fetched to your own disk; dropped files never leave the browser tab at all.

03

No tracking

No analytics, no telemetry, no third-party scripts phoning home. Your practice habits are yours alone.

04

Works offline

The waveform engine is vendored, not loaded from a CDN, so after the first track it keeps working with no internet.

05

Local storage only

Setlists, sessions, goals, streaks and themes all live in your browser's localStorage, on your machine.

06

Free & open source

Free to use and the full source is on GitHub: read it, fork it, run it yourself. No subscription, no expiry, no "service" that can disappear from under you.

14 · Under the hood

A small, honest stack.

A thin Flask backend fetches and streams the audio; everything else is vanilla JavaScript and the Web Audio API running in your browser. No bundler, no framework, no surprises.

BACKEND

Flask + yt-dlp

A tiny app factory fetches the track and streams it to the browser with HTTP range requests.

WAVEFORM

WaveSurfer.js v7

Vendored locally (no CDN) for the waveform, timeline and loop regions, so it works offline.

AUDIO

Web Audio API

A live graph powers pitch shift, channel isolation, EQ, the metronome, drone and note detection.

FFMPEG

Bundled

A bundled ffmpeg ships with the app, so there's nothing to install system-wide.

STORAGE

localStorage

Setlists, practice logs, goals and theme: all client-side, all on your machine.
